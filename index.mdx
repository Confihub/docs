Confi.cash is a shielded route to Stellar liquidity. You can use a private balance to swap through **Soroswap**, **Aquarius**, and other supported AMMs without publishing your individual order amount on-chain.

Your browser generates the zero-knowledge proof. Confi.cash combines eligible orders into an amount-blind batch, sends only the aggregate net to the selected AMM, and returns your proceeds as a fresh shielded note.

<Warning>
  Confi.cash is an experimental testnet proof of concept. Do not use real funds. It has not been independently audited.
</Warning>

## What Confi.cash protects

- Your shielded balance does not appear beside your public Stellar address.
- A shielded transfer sends a whole note to a `cb1...` address without publishing its amount.
- A swap through Soroswap, Aquarius, or another supported AMM hides your individual leg from chain observers. Only the batch net settles publicly on the selected AMM.
- Your spend keys and zero-knowledge proofs are created in your browser.

## What remains visible

- Deposits and withdrawals cross a public boundary. Their address, asset, and amount are visible on-chain.
- The chain shows that activity happened, when batches settled, the clearing price, and the batch net.
- The active batch path encrypts individual swap amounts before relay intake, but Confi.cash operates the threshold committee and can reconstruct them with quorum-level administrative access.
- The service operator can observe request metadata such as IP address and timing.
- A solo swap makes your individual amount the public batch net.

<Info>
  Confi.cash provides amount confidentiality against chain observers. It does not promise complete anonymity, invisibility, or protection from the current service operator.
</Info>
